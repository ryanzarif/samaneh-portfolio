Aesthetics of suffering (2014)

After the defection of the Green movement in Iran and the subsidence of Arab spring, I distinguished a wave of dark paintings in Iranian art galleries. Thus, my thesis was about to find the meaning of “suffering” in the field of sociology of art which resulted in an article based on Frankfort School's theories entitled “Aesthetics of suffering”. At the same time, I saw some of my colleagues were arrested in newspapers of was working at, for their political sight or cultural activities which were not tolerated by the government. We were under everyday pressure of horror of arresting while their blanked seats were bold. Therefore, I took photos of my colleagues’ portraits, printed and put those together such as Marelen Dumas artworks. Some of the squares are blanked in order to depict those blanked seats in that time. Some of those represent legs to depict hanged individuals.
