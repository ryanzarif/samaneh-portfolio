All around me (2012)

In “All around me” series I attempted to shed the light on the collective unconscious of Middle Eastern people who live in the constant pressure and horror of war. I sought to record the impact of their feelings in my mind by the George Lucas “Reflection theory”. In the “theory of the novel”, Lucas hypothesized that “human consciousness is formed through the perception of the outer world and, therefore, concepts and words [art] represent reflected images of reality in the human mind.” So, “All around me” depicts images had been subsided in my mind during Middle Eastern chaos which was widespread by media. My works were created by mixed media such as print, painting, and embroidery. Around that time I wrote a critique about the “For Aleppo” exhibition by Javad Modarresi.
